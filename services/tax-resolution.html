Facing the IRS? You Don't Have to Do It Alone.

Receiving an IRS notice, wage garnishment, or tax lien is frightening — but these problems are solvable. Our tax resolution specialists have helped clients reduce tax debt, stop collections, and get back in good standing with the IRS and state agencies.

With a signed Power of Attorney (Form 2848), we represent you directly before the IRS so you don't have to deal with them yourself.

Resolution Programs We Use

  • Installment Agreement: Monthly payment plan to pay your balance over time — we negotiate the most affordable terms
  • Offer in Compromise (OIC): Settle your debt for less than the full amount owed, based on your ability to pay
  • Partial Pay Installment Agreement: Pay a portion monthly; remaining balance may expire with the statute of limitations
  • Currently Not Collectible (CNC): Temporary hardship status that pauses IRS collection activity
  • Penalty Abatement: Request removal of penalties — First-Time Abatement is available if you have a clean prior record
  • Innocent Spouse Relief: Protection for spouses held liable for a partner's tax errors or fraud
  • Audit Representation: Full representation during IRS examinations and appeals

Common Situations We Resolve

  • Years of unfiled tax returns
  • Large balance due with mounting penalties and interest
  • IRS or state levy on bank accounts
  • Wage garnishment by the IRS
  • Federal or state tax lien on property
  • Business payroll tax problems (Trust Fund Recovery)
  • CP2000 notices (income discrepancy)
  • Audit notices and correspondence
⚠️ Act quickly: IRS collection actions escalate over time. The sooner you contact us after receiving a notice, the more options you have. Don't wait until a levy hits your bank account.

The Resolution Process

  1. Free Consultation: We review your notices, transcripts, and history
  2. Power of Attorney: We obtain your IRS records and communicate directly with the IRS on your behalf
  3. Strategy: We identify the best resolution path based on your financial situation
  4. Negotiation: We submit the appropriate application or agreement to the IRS
  5. Resolution: We confirm your agreement and ensure compliance going forward
